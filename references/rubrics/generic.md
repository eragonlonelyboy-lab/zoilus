# Rubric: generic

The fallback bar for any artifact with no specific rubric pack. Reject on doubt. The standard here is partly user-supplied: the bar for "good" depends on what this artifact is for. If the intended purpose or standard is unclear, ask for it before judging, do not invent one and grade against a guess.

## Blocking checks
- **Not fit for purpose.** The artifact must do the job it exists to do for the person who will use it. If it misses that job, or the job was never established, that is a blocking FAIL.
- **Incomplete.** A promised part is missing, a section is stubbed, a required input is absent. Gaps that stop the artifact from being used as-is are a FAIL.
- **Internally inconsistent.** Two parts contradict, a number in one place disagrees with the same number elsewhere, the structure fights itself. Any contradiction is a blocking gap.
- **Obvious errors.** Factual mistakes, broken references, malformed output, things that are simply wrong on inspection. One clear error is a FAIL.

## Quality checks
- Clears a world-class bar for its kind. Compare against the best version of this artifact type, not an average one.
- Right level of detail for the audience. Not too thin to act on, not padded past use.
- Skimmable and well-ordered. The reader finds what they need fast.
- No filler, no obvious slop, nothing that reads as unfinished or auto-generated.

## Verdict
FAIL if the artifact is not fit for purpose, is incomplete, contradicts itself, or contains an obvious error. If the intended standard was never clear, say so and ask, rather than passing or failing against an assumed bar.
