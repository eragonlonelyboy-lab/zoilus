# Rubric: prose

World-class bar for writing craft. Reject on doubt. This is about how the words work on the page, not whether the reader converts (that is copy-critique). Judge craft while preserving the author's voice. Do not flatten it into house style.

## Blocking checks
- **Boring or unclear enough to lose the reader.** If the intended reader would put this down before the end, out of tedium or confusion, that is a blocking FAIL. Name where they drop.
- **Filler and hedging that carry no weight.** "Very," "really," "just," "I think," "it could be argued," throat-clearing intros that say nothing. If cutting a phrase changes nothing, the phrase is a FAIL when it recurs.
- **Weak verbs and accidental passive voice.** "Is," "was," "has," nominalizations ("make a decision" for "decide"), passive constructions the author did not choose for effect. Buried actors are a FAIL.
- **A weak opening or closing line.** The first line must earn the second. The last line must land, not trail off. A flat open or a limp close is a blocking gap.

## Quality checks
- Rhythm varies. Not every sentence the same length and shape. Read it aloud in your head. Clunk is a defect.
- No walls of text. Dense blocks that should break get flagged. One idea per paragraph.
- Concrete over abstract. Specifics beat adjectives. Show the thing.
- Every sentence clear on first read. No re-reading to parse meaning.
- Voice intact. The edit sharpens the author, it does not replace them.

## Verdict
FAIL if the reader would not finish, if filler and weak verbs pervade, if the open or close falls flat, or if a sentence needs re-reading to parse. Otherwise note the tightening, and confirm the voice survived.
