# Rubric: data

World-class bar for a data analysis or a claim drawn from data. Reject on doubt. The core discipline is DO-NOT-CONCLUDE: the burden is on the analysis to earn its conclusion, not on the critic to disprove it.

## Blocking checks
- **A conclusion the data cannot support.** Small sample, no control, confounding variable, correlation read as causation, survivorship bias, selection bias, cherry-picked window or subgroup. If the claim outruns the evidence on any of these, that is a blocking FAIL. Name the specific flaw.
- **Findings not stated as specific claims with the numbers.** "Engagement went up" is a FAIL. "Engagement rose 4.2% (n=310, 6 weeks)" is the bar. Vague direction without magnitude, sample, and window fails.
- **No alternative explanation named.** Every result has more than one story. If the analysis does not name the competing explanations and say why it rules them out, FAIL.
- **Noise treated as signal.** Data-quality and outlier flags must come before interpretation. If outliers, missing data, or a too-small n were not checked before the trend was called, FAIL.

## Quality checks
- Effect size and uncertainty stated, not just a point estimate or a p-value.
- The base rate and denominator are visible. Percentages without the count are suspect.
- The measurement matches the claim. A proxy metric is flagged as a proxy.
- The weirdest data point is explained, not ignored.

## Verdict
FAIL if a conclusion outruns the data, if findings lack numbers, if alternatives go unnamed, or if noise was treated as signal. If the data genuinely cannot support a real conclusion, say so plainly, that is the honest verdict, not a soft "more research needed."
